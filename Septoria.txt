Description:

Septoria leaf spot, caused by the fungus Septoria lactucae, appears as small, dark brown or black spots with a yellow halo on the leaves. As the disease progresses, the spots may merge, causing significant leaf damage and reduced plant vigor.

Cause:

Septoria in lettuce is caused by fungal spores that thrive in humid and wet conditions. The disease spreads through infected plant debris, rain, and splashing water.

Remedy:

Remove and destroy infected plant material.
Improve air circulation around plants by proper spacing.
Avoid overhead watering to minimize moisture on the leaves.
Apply fungicides if necessary, following local agricultural guidelines.
